# Aurum - Advanced Filter Window Guide

The Advanced Filter Window in Aurum provides powerful tools for refining your item searches and market analysis. This guide covers how to access the window, use the various filters, manage presets, and tips for power users.

## 🌟 Accessing the Advanced Filter Window

You can open the Advanced Filter Window from the main Aurum dashboard. Look for the "Advanced Filters" button or icon (usually a funnel) near the search bar or filters section.

## 🛠️ Filter Categories

The window is organized into several collapsible sections to keep the interface clean while offering granular control.

### 1. Search
*   **Item Name**: Filter items by partial name match. This works in conjunction with other filters.

### 2. Basic Requirements
*   **Equip Level**: Define the required character level range to equip the item (e.g., 1-100).
    *   Useful for targeting specific expansion level caps (50, 60, 70, 80, 90, 100).
*   **Item Level (iLvl)**: Define the range for the item's distinct power level (e.g., 1-999).
    *   Essential for filtering endgame gear or specific tier sets.

### 3. Market & Profit
Customize your financial thresholds to find the most lucrative opportunities.
*   **Min Profit (Gil)**: Only show items where the expected profit (Price - Cost) is above this amount.
*   **Min ROI %**: Minimum Return on Investment percentage.
*   **Min Sales/Day (Velocity)**: Filter for items that sell at least this many times per day.
*   **Show Only Profitable**: A quick toggle to hide any items that are currently selling at a loss.

### 4. Categories

#### Jobs / Classes
Select which specific classes you want to see items for.
*   **All Jobs**: Default setting. Shows items for any crafter.
*   **Specific Selection**: Uncheck "All Jobs" to pick specific classes (CRP, BSM, ARM, GSM, LTW, WVR, ALC, CUL).

#### Equipment Slots
Filter by gear slot to target specific markets (e.g., Accessories often sell faster than Body armor).
*   **Left Side**: Main Hand, Head, Body, Hands, Legs, Feet.
*   **Right Side**: Off Hand, Ears, Neck, Wrists, Rings.

#### Item Sources
*   **Crafted Items**: Items created by players.
*   **Gathered Items**: Items from Mining, Botany, or Fishing.
*   **Vendor Items**: Items purchasable from NPCs.
*   **Market Tradeable**: Toggle to ensure you only see items that can be sold on the Market Board.
*   **Exclude Untradeable / Ex**: Hides EX trial drops or other untradeable items.

#### Item Types
Broad category toggles:
*   **Combat Gear**: Weapons and armor.
*   **Crafting & Gathering Gear**: Gear for DoH/DoL.
*   **Consumables**: Food and Potions.
*   **Crafting Materials**: Intermediate ingredients.
*   **Furniture & Housing**: Indoor and outdoor furnishings.

### 5. Item Properties
*   **Rarity (1-7)**: Filter by item color/rarity (Common, Uncommon, Rare, Relic, etc.).
*   **Min Materia Slots**: Find gear with guaranteed materia slots (e.g., Pentamelding bases).
*   **Dyeable Only**: Show only items that can be dyed.
*   **Collectable Only**: Show items related to the Collectables system.
*   **Exclude Unique**: Hides "Unique" items (items you can only hold one of).

## 💾 Managing Presets

Presets allow you to save complex filter configurations and switch between them instantly.

### Saving a Preset
1.  Configure your filters as desired in the "Current Filter" tab.
2.  Click the **"Save as Preset"** button at the bottom.
3.  Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Endgame Raid Food", "Leveling Gear 80-90").
4.  Click **"Save"**.

### Loading a Preset
1.  Switch to the **"Presets"** tab.
2.  Select a preset from the list.
    *   *Tip: Hover over a preset to see a quick summary of its settings.*
3.  Click **"Load Selected"**.
4.  The window will switch back to the "Current Filter" tab with your settings applied.

### Deleting a Preset
1.  Go to the **"Presets"** tab.
2.  Select the preset you wish to remove.
3.  Click **"Delete"**.

## 🚀 Best Practices for Power Users

1.  **Niche Targeting**: Combine **Job** + **Level** + **Item Type** filters.
    *   *Example*: "ALC" + "Level 90-100" + "Consumables" to dominate the endgame potion market.
2.  **Velocity is Key**: Don't just look at high prices. Set **Min Sales/Day** to at least `1.0` or `2.0` to ensure your stock moves.
3.  **Low-Hanging Fruit**: Use **Min ROI** > 100% and **Min Profit** > 1000g to find easy, low-risk flips or crafts.
4.  **Housing Specialist**: Create a preset for **Furniture & Housing** + **Include Market Tradeable**. Housing items often have high margins but lower velocity.
5.  **Clean Up**: Use **Exclude Untradeable** and **Exclude Unique** to keep your lists clean of items you can't easily mass-produce and sell.
